Configurable Rendering

Implemented a fully customizable rendering system, allowing fine-tuned control over environmental and visual parameters, including:

  • Lighting Conditions – Adjusting light count, intensity, position, temperature, and direction to simulate different real-world lighting scenarios.
  • Post-processing Effects – Configurable blur, contrast, saturation, and exposure settings to reflect document degradation, printing variations, and camera imperfections.
  • Camera Properties – Customizable resolution, angle, focal point, cropping, and rotation settings to replicate variations in document scans, photos, and captures.
  • Physical Deformations – Simulated document folds, creases, and warping effects, causing text to curve and shift, testing OCR resilience to real-world distortions.
  • Perforations & Holes – Integrated punched holes and document tears, obstructing portions of text and challenging annotation alignment.
  • Stains & Dirt Marks – Added water stains, ink smudges, and dirt marks to obscure text areas, evaluating how models handle text occlusion.
  • Faded & Blurred Text – Applied fading, ink bleed, and Gaussian blur to simulate aging or poor-quality printing, distorting character edges.
  • Annotation Impact – Ensured bounding boxes and segmentation masks adapt dynamically to distortions, improving dataset accuracy for real-world OCR training.

By incorporating these factors, the dataset improves model robustness, enabling AI to handle imperfect, real-world documents.
