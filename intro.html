Why am I a programmer?

I enjoy challenges, using mathematics and being creative. I get to indulge in each of those aspects whilst programming games. Not to mention, I love games and how they also share these qualities. Seeing someone experience one of my creations has to be one of the most rewarding feelings. The feedback I’ve received from people such as when I showcased at the Yorkshire Gaming Festival really helped me grow and understand my project. This is why I love programming, it allows me to develop as an individual whilst being part of a community that grows through facing challenges, working together and reinforcing productivity through feedback.

What am I up to now?

In my final year at the University of Huddersfield, having lots of fun developing in Unity and excited at the prospect of working in the games industry. Scroll down a bit to hear about what I'm working on right now...

My Individual Project

Currently working on a Unity tool for optimisation of in-game agents using genetic algorithms. By searching a Unity Prefab for it’s scripts and identifying modifiable variables I’m able to create a set of genes to manipulate. Then, once I have set a reasonable fitness function (to determine their success) I’m able to optimise the agents behaviour in relation to that fitness function. In my test case I intend to use a Goal Oriented Action Planning (GOAP) approach.

My Team Project

I’m also the project lead on a university team project where we are developing “Bind & Banish” a wacky first person ghost hunting game set in a haunted hotel. You must banish poltergeists from your world by harpooning, binding and blasting them from existence. Sounds absolutely bonkers? Just a little.
